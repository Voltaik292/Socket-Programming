Networks Under Attack

The Internet, while essential for modern life, is vulnerable to attacks by malicious actors who exploit weaknesses in networks and devices. Common threats include malware, such as viruses and spyware, which can infect devices, steal data, or turn them into botnets for large-scale attacks like distributed denial-of-service (DDoS). These attacks overwhelm servers or infrastructure, making them unusable through methods like bandwidth flooding, vulnerability exploits, or connection overloads.

Another major risk is packet sniffing, where attackers intercept unencrypted data transmitted over networks, especially in wireless environments, capturing sensitive information like passwords or private messages. Additionally, IP spoofing allows attackers to forge packet source addresses, impersonating trusted entities to deceive systems or users, highlighting the need for strong endpoint authentication mechanisms.

The Internet's original design assumed a trusted environment, leaving it inherently vulnerable in today's landscape of untrusted users and devices. Addressing these threats requires robust security measures, including encryption, authentication, and resilient network architectures, to defend against evolving attacks.

Common Attack Types:

  • Malware (Viruses, Spyware)
  • DDoS Attacks
  • Packet Sniffing
  • IP Spoofing

Defensive Measures:

  1. Use encryption (e.g., HTTPS, VPNs).
  2. Implement endpoint authentication.
  3. Deploy firewalls and intrusion detection systems.
